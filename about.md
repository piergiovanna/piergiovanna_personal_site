# About

## Professional and Academic Profile
My name is Piergiovanna Grossi, and my academic and professional work lies at the intersection of ancient studies and information technologies applied to Cultural Heritage. As a lecturer and researcher in the field of Digital Humanities, I am dedicated to developing innovative methodologies for the management, preservation, accessibility, and enhancement of historical and archaeological heritage through advanced digital tools. My research and professional activities also focus on digital accessibility, promoting inclusive approaches to the design and dissemination of cultural content and digital heritage resources.

## Teaching
I currently hold teaching positions at the University of Verona, where I teach workshops in Humanistic Computing, Web Design, and Digital Accessibility. My teaching and research activities focus primarily on digital accessibility, inclusive design, and the development of accessible digital environments for cultural heritage and education. I also collaborate with other universities and research institutions on projects and teaching activities related to Digital Humanities, exploring the application of emerging technologies, including Large Language Models (LLMs) and artificial intelligence, to cultural heritage, archaeology, and classical studies.

## Research
My research is characterised by a strong commitment to digital accessibility, inclusive access to cultural heritage, and the principles of open knowledge. This translates into the integration of Wikimedia projects, open technologies, and digital methodologies into research, teaching, and public engagement activities. I have coordinated and contributed to numerous digital heritage projects, focusing on data modelling, information management, and the development of web-based tools for the dissemination and accessibility of cultural resources. I also collaborate with universities, museums, and cultural institutions on research, teaching, and digital innovation initiatives in the fields of Digital Humanities and Cultural Heritage.

## Digital Culture and Civic Engagement
Beyond the strictly academic sphere, I actively promote the dissemination of digital culture, open knowledge and free software as tools for democracy and participation. I <!-- am among the founders of CoderDojo Padova, where I coordinate activities aimed at developing computational thinking in younger generations, and I --> collaborate with organisations such as Wikimedia Italia and Gfoss.it (Associazione per l'Informazione Geografica Libera) to foster the adoption of open standards and ensure the broadest possible accessibility of knowledge within cultural institutions and public administration.
