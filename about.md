<h2 id="profile">Profile</h2>

My academic and professional work lies at the intersection of historical and archaeological studies, Digital Humanities, and digital technologies applied to Cultural Heritage.

My primary interests include digital accessibility, cultural heritage, digital archaeology, and open knowledge, with the goal of making cultural resources more accessible, inclusive, and reusable. I am also interested in archaeological information systems, Wikimedia projects, open-source technologies, and the application of artificial intelligence to the humanities.

---

<h2 id="teaching">Teaching</h2>

At the University of Verona, I teach courses and workshops in Humanistic Computing, Web Design, and Digital Accessibility.

I also collaborate with universities, research centres, and cultural institutions on educational initiatives related to Digital Humanities, accessibility, cultural heritage communication, and the use of emerging technologies in the humanities.

Key teaching and training topics include:

* Digital Humanities
* Digital Accessibility
* Web Design and Web Publishing
* Digital Cultural Heritage
* Wikimedia, Wikidata, and Wikimedia Commons
* Open Data and Open Science
* GIS and Geospatial Technologies for Cultural Heritage
* Artificial Intelligence for the Humanities

<h2 id="research">Research</h2>

My research focuses on developing methodologies and tools for the management, enhancement, and accessibility of cultural heritage.

In recent years, my work has focused particularly on:

* Digital Accessibility and Cultural Heritage
* Digital Humanities
* Digital Archaeology
* Archaeological Information Systems
* GIS and WebGIS for Cultural Heritage
* Open Data and Open Knowledge
* Wikimedia and GLAM Projects
* Public Archaeology
* Cultural Heritage Communication and Dissemination

I have participated in and coordinated research projects dedicated to archaeological data modelling, information system interoperability, knowledge dissemination through collaborative platforms, and the promotion of open access to cultural resources.

I currently collaborate with universities, museums, archives, libraries, and cultural institutions on initiatives related to accessibility, Digital Humanities, and cultural heritage enhancement.

---

<h2 id="digital-culture-and-civic-engagement">Digital Culture and Civic Engagement</h2>

Alongside my academic work, I actively promote digital culture, open knowledge, and free and open-source software as tools for participation, inclusion, and active citizenship.

I collaborate with Wikimedia Italia, GFOSS.it (the Italian Association for Free Geographic Information), and other organizations committed to promoting open standards, open data, and collaborative practices for the creation and sharing of knowledge.

I am actively involved in Wikimedia initiatives dedicated to cultural heritage, including:

* Wikipedia and Archaeology
* GLAM – Civic Museums of Reggio Emilia
* GLAM – Veneto Toy Museum

I also contribute to initiatives focused on accessibility within Wikimedia projects and collaborate with the DAIH (Digital Arena for Inclusive Humanities) at the University of Verona on topics related to inclusion, accessibility, and digital technologies for the humanities.
